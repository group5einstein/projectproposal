PROJECT PROCESS OVERVIEW

Conceptualize Project:
The initial phase involves brainstorming and defining the goals, objectives, and scope of the "Chore Website." Identify the target audience, key features, and the value proposition it offers to both employers and job seekers.

Our target audience includes busy individuals, such as working professionals, parents, and individuals with multiple responsibilities, who are seeking assistance with their household chores and tasks. Additionally, we aim to cater to elderly or disabled individuals who require support in completing various chores.

The key features of chore website involve chore listings, user profiles, search and filters, messaging and communication tools, site maps, payment and compensation systems, privacy and security measures, and mobile accessibility. These features enable users to create and browse chore listings, connect with chore providers, communicate securely, manage tasks efficiently, ensure fair compensation, and access the platform conveniently from mobile devices. It is valuable for both businesses and job seekers because it offers a simple way to link those in need of assistance with those wishing to earn income or offer services. Having a pool of trustworthy and suitable applicants to choose from gives employers an advantage because it makes it simpler for them to assign duties and reduces their workload. On the other hand, job seekers can discover flexible employment options that match their abilities, schedule, and financial needs. By bringing these two parties together, the CHORE website simplifies the chore outsourcing process and creates a mutually beneficial environment.

Researching Available Data:
We shall research existing chore-related platforms, analyze market trends, and gather insights to ensure the "Chore Website" addresses users' pain points and offers innovative solutions. Identify gaps and opportunities to ensure that the website addresses them effectively.

Setting Deadlines and Meetings:
We will establish realistic deadlines for each phase of the project. Schedule regular meetings with group members to discuss progress, address challenges, and make necessary adjustments.

Assigning Tasks:
We are going to divide the project into manageable tasks and assign responsibilities to group members based on their expertise and availability. Clearly define roles, ensuring everyone understands their responsibilities and deliverables.

Finding a web or blog host:
We are committed to select a reliable web hosting provider that offers the necessary features and scalability to support the website. Ensure the host allows for secure user data storage and smooth website performance.

Creating a site map:
We intend to develop a well-organized site map that will outline the website's structure, including main pages, categories, and subcategories. This will enable users to quickly find the information they need, whether they are employers looking to post chore requests or job seekers searching for available opportunities. A clear and intuitive site map contributes to user satisfaction and encourages frequent usage of the platform.

Listing necessary applications and web apps:
These are job listing platforms, applicant tracking systems, a chore posting and search feature, communication tools, and payment integration.

Funding (if applicable):
We plan to determine the financial requirements of the project. Explore funding options such as grants, investors, or partnerships to ensure adequate resources for website development.

DEVELOPMENT
Creation of the Website:
During this phase, Our group will design and develop the "Chore Website." This includes a visually appealing and intuitive website that showcases job opportunities and allows job seekers to create profiles and submit applications.

RELEASE AND PROMOTION
Pre-release promotion:
We will start promoting the chore website before its official launch and use email campaigns, social media platforms, and partnerships to create anticipation and attract both employers and job seekers.

Actual Website Release:
Once development is complete, We will thoroughly test the website to ensure a seamless user experience and fix any issues or bugs identified during testing. After rigorous quality assurance, We will release the "Chore Website" for public view, ensuring it is accessible on multiple devices and browsers.

MAINTENANCE
Responding to feedback: We will actively listen to user feedback and respond promptly, address concerns, inquiries, and suggestions from both employers and job seekers to continuously improve the platform's functionality and user experience. Continuous improvement: We will regularly evaluate the performance of the website, analyze user behavior, update content, and optimize the website.
